You Are Not Alone

If you or someone you know has experienced gender-based violence, help is available. You deserve support, respect, and safety. Healing is possible, and there are people ready to walk with you on this journey.

What To Do If You Experience GBV

1. Ensure Your Immediate Safety

  • Move to a safe location away from the perpetrator if possible
  • Call emergency services (999, 911, or 112) if you're in immediate danger
  • Reach out to a trusted friend, family member, or neighbor
  • If you need to leave home, contact a shelter or safe house

2. Seek Medical Attention

  • Visit a hospital or health center as soon as possible, even if injuries seem minor
  • Medical professionals can document injuries, provide treatment, and offer Post-Exposure Prophylaxis (PEP) to prevent HIV if needed (must be taken within 72 hours)
  • Request a P3 form (medical examination form) which can be used as evidence
  • GVRC and other centers provide free medical services for survivors

3. Preserve Evidence

  • If possible, don't wash, change clothes, or clean up before medical examination
  • Keep any torn clothing, messages, emails, or photos as evidence
  • Write down details of what happened while it's fresh in your memory
  • Medical professionals can help collect forensic evidence

4. Report to Authorities

  • Visit the nearest police station, preferably one with a gender desk
  • You have the right to file a report and be treated with dignity
  • Request a female officer if that makes you more comfortable
  • You can bring a support person with you
  • Get an Occurrence Book (OB) number for your report

5. Get Emotional Support

  • Contact a counselor or support organization - healing takes time
  • Join a support group to connect with other survivors
  • Don't blame yourself - what happened is not your fault
  • Healing is not linear, and all your feelings are valid

6. Know Your Legal Rights

  • You can access free legal aid from organizations like FIDA Kenya
  • You can apply for a protection order to keep the perpetrator away
  • You have the right to withdraw a case, though it's best to discuss this with a counselor or lawyer
  • The Sexual Offences Act (2006) protects survivors and prosecutes offenders
Remember: You are not alone. What happened is not your fault. You deserve support, healing, and justice. Take things one step at a time, and reach out for help whenever you need it.

About This Resource

This website provides information and resources for survivors of gender-based violence in Kenya. Our goal is to connect you with trusted organizations that can provide immediate help, ongoing support, and pathways to healing.

Gender-based violence includes physical, sexual, emotional, and economic abuse. It affects people of all ages, backgrounds, and circumstances. If you've experienced any form of violence, you deserve compassionate care and support.

You are believed. You are supported. You are not alone.
